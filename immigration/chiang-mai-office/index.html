Chiang Mai Immigration: Two Offices, One Digital System

Home / Immigration / Chiang Mai Immigration Offices . Updated May 2026 . 8 min read

In 2026, Chiang Mai immigration operates two separate offices serving different purposes. Know which one handles your visa type, and you'll avoid wasted trips. Get it wrong, and you're back tomorrow.

TL;DR: Quick Routing

ED, DTV initial, retirement, marriage, business, TM30, residence certificate: go to Airport Road. Tourist extension, 90-day reporting, re-entry permit, DTV 180-day extension, passport transfer: go to Central Festival. TDAC digital verification is mandatory for all. Queue times: Airport Road 7.30 AM (arrive before 8.00 AM), Central Festival 8.15 AM for mall opening.

Airport Road: The Main Office

71 Moo 3, Sanambin Road, Suthep Sub-District, Muang District, Chiang Mai 50200

Hours: 08:00–16:00. Lunch 12:00–13:00. Closes 15:30 (stops accepting new cases). Open weekdays only. Closed Thai public holidays.

Services:

  • ED Visa (initial applications and annual renewals)
  • DTV Visa (initial applications, NOT 180-day extensions)
  • Retirement Visa (Non-O)
  • Marriage Visa (Non-O) — spouse must attend
  • Business Visa (Non-B) extensions and work permits
  • TM.30 residence address notification (digital and paper)
  • Residence Certificate (formal document for driving license, vehicle purchase)
  • Re-entry Permits (single or multiple)
Guru Tip: The Queue Number Game

Arrive at 07:30 AM, before the gate opens at 08:00. Immigration issues queue numbers by 08:15 AM. If you arrive at 08:30 or 09:00, you might be number 40+. Office processes 3-4 people per hour. Do the maths. A low queue number (under 20) means 5-7 hours. A high number (40+) means 10+ hours or next day. Bring a book.

Central Festival: The Convenience Branch

2nd Floor, Central Chiangmai Mall, near Supersports zone, Superhighway

Hours: 09:00–17:00. Lunch break 12:00–13:00. Service until 16:30. Open weekdays only. Closed Thai public holidays.

Services:

  • Tourist Visa extensions (30-day or approval letter)
  • 90-Day Reporting (TM.28)
  • Re-entry Permits (single 1,000 THB or multiple 3,800 THB)
  • Passport Transfer (moving visa stamps from old to new passport)
  • DTV 180-day local extensions (not initial applications)

Central Festival is faster because volumes are lower. 90-day reporting: typically 5-10 minutes. Tourist extensions: 20-30 minutes. No queue number system. First-come, first-served. A line forms at the mall entrance by 08:15 AM (mall doesn't open until 10 AM officially, but immigration staff start at 09:00).

TDAC: The Digital Checkpoint You Can't Skip

Thailand Digital Arrival Control. In 2026, every long-stay visa extension is cross-checked against this system. Your landlord or condo manager must have filed your TM.30 (residential address notification) into TDAC within 24 hours of your arrival or address change.

If your TM.30 is not in the system, immigration will reject your extension. They won't process it anyway. So call your landlord before you go. Say: "Please verify that my TM.30 has been uploaded to TDAC. Please show me the confirmation." If it hasn't, ask them to do it immediately. It takes 10 minutes. TDAC verification can take 1-2 days to appear in immigration's system.

Queue Strategy: Mainstream vs Deep Cut

Mainstream: Arrive 07:30 AM at Airport Road if you have ED, DTV initial, or long-stay applications. Get queue number under 20. Expect 5-7 hour wait. Bring lunch, phone charger, book. Bring all documents. Do not assume you have everything. Missing one document = full restart.

Deep Cut: File an online appointment through the official Chiang Mai Immigration website. Appointments available 2 weeks in advance. You bypass the queue entirely. Takes 2 weeks planning, but saves 5 hours. Best move if your visa is time-critical.

The Reality of Immigration Queues

Thai immigration offices are busy, especially in popular expat towns like Chiang Mai. Peak periods can mean 2–4 hour waits. But queues aren't random. They follow predictable patterns, and there are ways to avoid them.

The good news: many filings can be done entirely online. And if you must visit in person, timing is everything.

Best Days and Times to Visit

Immigration queues are directly tied to reporting deadlines. Understanding when most people file gives you a clear advantage.

🟢 Best Days: 1–9 of Month

Quietest period. Most people file mid to late month. Early month is your best window for a short queue.

🟡 Moderate: 10–25 of Month

Peak filing window. People trying to meet deadlines. Expect 1–3 hour waits, especially mid-week.

🟢 Good Days: 26–30 of Month

Second-best window. People filing just before month's end. Still quieter than mid-month.

⏰ Time Matters: Before 9 AM

Morning arrivals beat afternoon crowds. Queue numbers are lowest 8:30–9:30 AM, peak again at noon.

Skip the Queue: File Online

The fastest way to beat immigration queues is to not show up at all. Both TM.47 (90-day reporting) and TM30 (address registration) can be filed entirely online.

💡 Pro Tip: Online is Official

The TDAC and TM30 online systems are official government channels. Filing online has the same legal weight as in-person filing. No need for a TM.47 or TM30 proof print unless specifically requested during an extension.

If You Must Visit: Strategy

Not all filings can be done online yet. Visa extensions, re-entry permits, and certain special cases still require a visit. Here's how to make it painless.

Before You Go

  • Check the weather and seasons. Hot season (March–May) and festivals bring tourist visa extensions. Avoid during major holidays.
  • Pre-file online if possible. TM.47 and TM30 online reduce the burden on offices during peak days.
  • Bring two complete sets of documents. Immigration staff make photocopies. Having duplicates ready speeds things up.
  • Arrive at 8:30 AM. Queue numbers are handed out until 9:30 or 10 AM. Getting there early guarantees you are in the system for the day.
  • Bring your phone fully charged. You may need to pay fees via QR code or PromptPay while waiting.

📋 Pre-Visit Checklist

  • All required documents photocopied (2 sets)
  • Passport original + copies (data page and last entry stamp)
  • TM.6 or TM.7 form (completed, if extension)
  • House registration or rental agreement (for address verification)
  • Proof of funds (if required by visa type)
  • Application form filled out neatly in pen
  • Phone charged (for QR payments)
  • Sunscreen and water (queues can be long)

While You are There

  • Take a queue number as soon as you arrive. Even if the office opens at 8:30, take your number immediately. This locks you into the system.
  • Sit down and wait. Don't hover near the counter or desks—it slows staff processing. Sit down, check your phone, and wait for your number.
  • When your number is called, have documents ready. Don't fumble through your bag. Have everything in order in a folder.
  • Answer questions directly. If staff ask about your work or address, answer factually. Don't volunteer extra information that could raise red flags.
  • Confirm next steps before leaving. Ask exactly when your extension or permit will be ready and whether you need to return.

Why Queues Spike Mid-Month

90-day reporting deadlines create predictable queue patterns:

  • TM.47 deadline is 15 days before your 90 days are up, or 7 days after.
  • Most people file between days 15–22 of the month (roughly matching the 15-day-before window).
  • Visa extensions follow a similar pattern, with people filing just before their stamp expires.
  • Early month is quiet because few people have deadlines on days 1–9.

Chiang Mai Immigration Offices

Main Office

Location: 71 Moo 3, Airport Road, Suthep Sub-district, Muang District, Chiang Mai 50200 (handles all immigration services).

Hours: Monday–Friday, 8:30 AM–4:30 PM. Closed Thai public holidays.

Contact: 053-201755, Call Center 1178

Getting there: Grab or taxi from Old City. Allow 20–30 minutes travel time.

Central Festival Secondary Office (Limited Services)

Location: Central Festival shopping mall, Floor 2, Door 2/1 (SuperSport zone side), Chiang Mai-Lampang Highway, Amphoe Mueang, Chiang Mai 50000.

Hours: Monday–Friday, 9:00 AM–12:00 PM, 1:00 PM–5:00 PM.

Services: Short-term visa extension (tourist), family visa, re-entry permits, 90-day reporting, and selected other services. Services are limited and subject to change. Verify availability before visiting.

Contact: 053-201755

Visa Types

Long Stay Visa Categories

Each card below links to a dedicated guide for that visa type. All guides include eligibility, financial requirements, renewal rules, and Chiang Mai-specific immigration notes.

🎓

Education Visa

6–12 months

Study Thai language, martial arts, Muay Thai, or other programs at accredited institutions. Extensions available every 3 months.

ED Visa guide →
🏡

Retirement Visa

1 year (renewable)

For retirees aged 50+ with passive income or savings. Straightforward requirements and low-cost option for settling long-term.

Retirement guide →
💍

Marriage Visa

1 year (renewable)

For those married to a Thai national. Requires documentation of the relationship and financial proof.

Marriage guide →
💼

Business Visa

3 months–1 year

For entrepreneurs and business owners. Requires company registration and documentation showing Thai business investment.

Business guide →
🌍

DTV (Destination Thailand)

5 years

New visa for digital nomads, remote workers, and freelancers. 180 days per entry, multiple entries, no local work permitted.

DTV guide →
🏆

LTR (Long-Term Resident)

10 years

Premium visa for professionals, remote workers, and high-income earners. Includes work permit and government support.

LTR guide →
💎

Thailand Privilege Elite

5–20 years

Premium membership program with visa, work permit, and concierge support. Highest cost option with maximum flexibility.

Elite guide →
📊

Investment Visa

1 year (renewable)

For investors in Thai businesses or real estate. Requires significant capital commitment and documentation.

Investment guide →
🆚

Volunteer Visa

1 year (renewable)

For volunteering with registered Thai NGOs and organizations. Low-cost option with flexibility for volunteer work.

Volunteer guide →

Visa requirements, financial thresholds, and processing rules are updated annually by Thai immigration. Always verify current requirements with official sources or contact us before applying.

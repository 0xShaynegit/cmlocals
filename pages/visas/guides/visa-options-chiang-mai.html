Thai Visa Options in Chiang Mai

Local guide to visa options for Chiang Mai residents. Chiang Mai Immigration Office procedures, which visas are easiest to apply for locally, practical tips, and contact details.

Last verified: March 2026
Home / Visas / Guides / Visa Options in Chiang Mai

Chiang Mai Immigration Office: Location & Hours

Chiang Mai Immigration Office handles all visa applications, extensions, and renewals for Chiang Mai province residents and visitors.

  • Location: Mae Rim District, approximately 30 minutes north of Chiang Mai city center
  • Address: 143/2 Moo 2, Salee Sub-district, Mae Rim District, Chiang Mai 50180
  • Operating hours: Monday–Friday, 8:30 AM – 4:30 PM (lunch break typically 12:00–1:00 PM)
  • Contact: +66 (0) 53-286-000 (ext. for visa services)
  • Website: www.immigration.go.th (for national procedures and forms)
  • Tip: Arrive early (before 10 AM) for faster processing. Bring all documents in duplicate

Language support: English speakers are available at Chiang Mai Immigration, but bring a translator or Thai-speaking friend if you're unfamiliar with visa terminology. Having both Thai and English documents helps.

Easiest Visas to Apply for in Chiang Mai

DTV (Digital Nomad Visa) – Apply Abroad Only

The DTV must be applied for at a Thai embassy or consulate outside Thailand. Chiang Mai residents typically apply at Bangkok's Thai Embassy or at a consulate in their home country before arrival. Once in Chiang Mai on DTV, renewal happens at Chiang Mai Immigration.

Retirement Visa (Non-O) – Relatively Easy Locally

Retiring in Chiang Mai? You can extend or renew retirement visas at Chiang Mai Immigration. If starting a new Retirement visa, apply at the Thai Embassy in your home country, then manage renewals in Chiang Mai.

  • Chiang Mai Immigration handles renewals smoothly with consistent staff
  • Processing time: 1–3 days for renewal
  • Required each year: Updated bank statement showing 800,000 THB or income letter (65,000 THB/month)

Marriage Visa (Non-O) – Local Application Possible

If married to a Thai national and already in Thailand, you can apply for initial Marriage visa at Chiang Mai Immigration. Many Chiang Mai residents use this route successfully.

  • Thai spouse must accompany you to immigration
  • Marriage certificate (Thai translation required)
  • Processing: 1–2 weeks for initial application, 1–3 days for renewal
  • Renewal is routine if financial and co-signature requirements maintained

Tourist Visa Extension – Very Common in Chiang Mai

Chiang Mai is a major hub for short-term visitors extending tourist visas. If you're on a tourist visa and want to extend, Chiang Mai Immigration processes thousands of these annually.

  • Processing time: 1 day (often same-day in low-traffic periods)
  • Cost: 1,900 THB
  • Required: Passport, TM.7 form (available on-site), arrival card photocopy

Bridge with education: Many Chiang Mai newcomers combine Thai language ED visas for initial 90-day stays, then transition to longer-term visas (DTV, Retirement, Marriage) afterward. This gives you time to settle before committing to extended residence.

Visa by Situation: Chiang Mai Edition

Retiring in Chiang Mai (Age 50+)

  • Best path: Apply for Retirement visa at Thai Embassy in your country, then manage renewals at Chiang Mai Immigration
  • Why Chiang Mai: Cost of living supports retirement lifestyle; immigration office is efficient and familiar with retirees
  • Local resource: Chiang Mai cost of living guide for retirement budgeting
  • Pro tip: Once settled, use Chiang Mai Immigration for all renewals—staff are experienced with long-term retirees

Remote Workers & Digital Nomads

  • Best path: Apply for DTV visa at Thai Embassy abroad, renew at Chiang Mai Immigration annually
  • Why Chiang Mai: Low cost, good internet infrastructure, co-working community, friendly visa renewal process
  • Local resource: Digital Nomads in Chiang Mai guide with co-working spaces and community
  • Alternative: Start with ED visa for skill-building (language, martial arts), then transition to DTV

Married to a Thai National

  • Best path: Initial Marriage visa at Chiang Mai Immigration (if already in Thailand), then annual renewals locally
  • Why Chiang Mai: Efficient processing, low-stress atmosphere, experienced staff with marriage visa renewals
  • Pro tip: Bring your Thai spouse to all appointments; co-signature is mandatory for renewals

Students & Education-Focused Visitors

  • Best path: Apply for ED visa through a school in Chiang Mai
  • Why Chiang Mai: Multiple language schools, martial arts camps, dance studios, and self-defense programs all support ED visa enrollment
  • Local options: Thai language schools, Muay Thai gyms, self-defense programs
  • Timeline: ED visa is valid 90 days; renewable if you continue your course

Mandatory Procedures for All Long-Stay Visa Holders in Chiang Mai

Regardless of which visa you hold, you must follow these Thailand-wide procedures through Chiang Mai Immigration:

  • 90-day Reporting: Report address and status every 90 days. Can be done in person or online at immigration office
  • TM30 (Address Registration): Landlord or accommodation must file within 24 hours of your arrival. Verify it's done or file yourself if landlord doesn't
  • Annual Visa Renewal: Plan renewal 1–2 months before expiry. Bring passport, financial proof, TM.47 form (available at Chiang Mai Immigration)

Get support: Full Chiang Mai Immigration Guide for step-by-step procedures, common mistakes, and compliance requirements.

Practical Tips for Chiang Mai Visa Management

  • Mark your calendar: Set phone reminders for 90-day reporting and visa renewal dates
  • Document organization: Keep copies of passport, visa, TM30, financial proof, and approval letters in a safe place
  • Translation services: Many documents need Thai translation. Chiang Mai has reliable translation services near the immigration office
  • Know the staff: Building familiarity with Chiang Mai Immigration makes renewals faster and less stressful
  • Community support: Join Chiang Mai expat groups online for shared visa experiences and procedural tips
